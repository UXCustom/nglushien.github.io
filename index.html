A Little About Me

I've been working in the web design/development industry for more than 11 years. My career began as a layout designer and flash animator. From there I became a front-end developer where I also worked on videography and post production. Currently I work on CMS template development. This gives me the experience of working for a large corporation with complex team structures and development environments. Over the last few years I've shifted my focus to UX design. The field combines my love of psychology with my passion for design. This is an exciting time to be in this industry and I'm glad to be a part of it.

My Passions

Research
UX is not just UI

Visual design is only one aspect of UX. I enjoy investigating projects systematically. Research can provide context into the design process. It better helps the designer understand the user, their needs and identifies requirements. UX research assists with problem detection and helps find resolutions.

Design
The UI/UX Side of Things

To provide a good interface the designer must understand the users needs. A good interface helps the user complete tasks. Graphic design is used to support how the user is going to interact with the product. An aesthetically appealing site could enhance or detract the ability of your users to interact with your site.

Development
Bringing It All Together

Proper planning is crucial at this stage. Bloated, overweight sites with slow load times will detract users. Having a site built on a solid foundation will ensure scalability. The front end development process is the culmination of markup, styling, functionality, QA, Accessibility, Usability and Performance.
